Our Hypothesis & Vission

Major Problems

The digital landscape of Pakistan (a developing country) has three major issues:

  • Problem 1: Dependence on Foreign Technologies

    Our tech ecosystem heavily relies on software and tools developed by other countries, limiting local innovation and reducing digital independence.

  • Problem 2: Uncritical Adoption Without Cultural Context

    We often adopt foreign technologies and practices without assessing their societal impact or alignment with our cultural and Islamic valuesâ€”risking unintended consequences.

  • Problem 3: Eroding Digital Sovereignty

    This increasing reliance compromises our ability to build self-sustaining systems and may jeopardize the freedom and autonomy of future generations.

Our Solutions

If Allah wills, We plan to take following three major steps to solve these problems:

  • Solution 1: Empowering Youth Through Purposeful Digital Awareness

    Foster digital literacy among youth to inspire early, meaningful contributions to societyâ€”grounded in responsibility, ethics, and long-term impact.

  • Solution 2: Collaborative Open-Source Innovation for Local Needs

    Bring together industry leaders, educators, technologists, and learners to co-create open-source solutions tailored for localized challenges and sustainable development.

  • Solution 3: Ethical and Critical Evaluation of Technology

    Conduct in-depth studies and real-world experiments to assess technological impact, identify potential risks, and ensure alignment with Islamic values and societal wellbeing.
